What is the dividends per share?
The dividends per share (DPS) is the total amount of dividends that belongs to each individual share outstanding of the firm. The dividends per share allows the investors to determine how much income the investor will receive from the company on a per share basis. The dividends are cash paid to investors in a company and there are other types of payment that can be received from the company.
How to calculate dividends per share?
To calculate dividends per share you should use the following formula:
dividends per share = total dividends / shares outstanding
Example: Company x paid $60,000 to shareholders in the previous quarter and the company has 20,000 outstanding shares, calcualte the dividends per share?
Using the dividends per share formula the answer will be:
dividends per share = total dividends / shares outstanding
dividends per share = $60,000 / 20,000
dividends per share = $3
Why use dividends per share?
The dividends per share is used by investors because it allows them to determine how much dividend payments they will receive per share of their common stock.
